Advertising Positions, 2017
Animations / Mixed-media Installation
Daniel C Howe

There was a time, however brief, when the web was a space largely free of commerce and capitalism. Today, however, our online actions are tracked by sophisticated automated systems ("robots") that mine our personal data to analyze, predict, and finally, determine our behavior. While this vast surveillance architecture has indeed been used by governments as a means of control, its unchecked growth is due largely to corporate interests. The system's human users are rarely asked to consent, but must instead accept tracking as the necessary cost of access to the “free” information we can no longer live without.

Advertising Positions began with “robots”, though with a different agenda. Rather than tracking humans, these robots were trained to search the web as if they were human, following specific profiles they were assigned (age, gender, location, interests, etc). Each trained robot was released on the web, driving a modified version of the Chrome browser, to periodically visit sites that it “liked”, and to click on links it chose, then bringing it to additional sites. This process continued over several weeks as the robots collected and saved each ad they were served.

The specific ads shown on websites are determined by real-time auctions, which occur within the time it takes the page to load, in which the robot's growing data profile is sold to the highest corporate bidder, who then injects one or more ads into the page content. The robot, rather than using these ads to make purchases, instead uses each as the texture for a single polygon on its virtual mesh body. The training period concludes when the robot’s new skin was complete.

In current iterations of the project, human volunteers are 3d-scanned in our laboratory and given custom software to collect the advertisements with which they are targeted over several months of web browsing (generally several thousand or more ads). Each ad is then applied to a single polygon on the mesh skin of the 3d avatar we create for them, animated according to their specific style of movement.

A fully-articulated conceptual art intervention with an aesthetic outcome… that at the same time operates as an engaged computationally‐sophisticated (programmatic generative bot-art) and polemical intervention against the ubiquitous networked advertising that permeates and ideologically colonizes cognitive space with visual trash: portraiture for the blemished flesh of browsing beings.

-D.J. Johnston
